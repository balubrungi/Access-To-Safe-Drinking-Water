Introduction

This six-week CSP project improves access to safe drinking water in a rural village through community monitoring, household treatment and safe storage, source protection and behaviour-change campaigns. Below are daily reports for each week with photos and an option to add one image per week.

Week 1 ‚Äî Planning & Baseline Survey (Daily)

Initial visits, meetings, mapping and volunteer selection. Focus: prepare for field testing.

Day 1 ‚Äî Village Intro & Meeting

Introduced the project to village leaders, explained objectives and planned schedule.

Day 2 ‚Äî Water Point Mapping

Mapped handpumps, wells and piped points; recorded GPS and visible risks.

Day 3 ‚Äî Baseline Household Survey

Collected data on water sources, treatment habits and recent water-related illnesses.

Day 4 ‚Äî Volunteer Selection

Recruited 6 community volunteers, prioritizing women and youth for inclusivity.

Day 5 ‚Äî Kit Preparation

Checked water-testing kits, sample bottles and arranged transport for field activities.

Day 6 ‚Äî Community Notice

Posted the weekly schedule on the noticeboard and informed households of upcoming tests and demos.

Day 7 ‚Äî Week 1 Report

Compiled baseline findings and prepared materials for Week 2 field testing.

Week 2 ‚Äî Field Testing & Sample Collection (Daily)

Collected water samples from various sources and performed field tests to evaluate water quality.

Day 1 ‚Äî Training & Briefing

Briefed volunteers on safe sampling procedures and testing methods.

Day 2 ‚Äî Sample Collection

Collected samples from borewells, handpumps and wells to check turbidity and pH levels.

Day 3 ‚Äî Testing & Analysis

Tested water for physical and chemical parameters and documented results.

Day 4 ‚Äî Repeat after rain

Retested sources after rainfall to check turbidity changes and record findings.

Day 5 ‚Äî Community Feedback

Shared preliminary results with residents; collected observations about taste and smell.

Day 6 ‚Äî Advise precautions

Advised residents to avoid using turbid sources until treated and recommended safe alternatives.

Day 7 ‚Äî Weekly summary

Compiled week‚Äôs data and prepared for Week 3 household demonstrations.

Week 3 ‚Äî Household Treatment & Safe Storage (Daily)

Demonstrations of boiling, SODIS and chlorination; household visits to encourage safe storage.

Day 1 ‚Äî Demonstration Setup

Set up demonstration area at school; prepared SODIS bottles and boiling station.

Day 2 ‚Äî Boiling Demo

Showed proper boiling duration and safe cooling practices to families.

Day 3 ‚Äî Chlorination Training

Explained safe dosage and handling of chlorine tablets; distributed sample tablets.

Day 4 ‚Äî Home Visits

Visited households to help them set up safe-storage containers and ladles.

Day 5 ‚Äî Taste Feedback

Collected taste and acceptability feedback after households tried treatment methods.

Day 6 ‚Äî Reinforcement

Reinforced instructions for households facing difficulties and retaught SODIS steps.

Day 7 ‚Äî Week 3 Wrap-up

Updated adoption logs and prepared notes for source protection activities next week.

Week 4 ‚Äî Source Protection & Rainwater Harvesting (Daily)

Clean-up drives, fencing, small recharge pits and a school rain-harvest pilot to protect sources and boost supply.

Day 1 ‚Äî Source Survey

Inspected wells and handpumps for litter, animal access and erosion.

Day 2 ‚Äî Clean-up Drive

Organised villagers to clear waste and desilt around the primary well.

Day 3 ‚Äî Fencing & Signage

Installed fencing and signs to protect the well from animals and waste.

Day 4 ‚Äî Rainwater Barrel Pilot

Installed rooftop diverter and barrel at school to collect rainwater for gardening.

Day 5 ‚Äî Planting & Mulch

Planted shrubs and applied mulch to reduce runoff near sources.

Day 6 ‚Äî School Demo

Explained how collected rainwater can be used for gardening and cleaning at school.

Day 7 ‚Äî Report & Handover

Compiled week photos and notes and handed over simple maintenance tips to caretakers.

Poster campaigns, street plays, quizzes and door-to-door outreach to encourage safe-water habits.

Day 1 ‚Äî Posters & Pamphlets

Designed and distributed local-language posters urging boil/store/protect practices.

Day 2 ‚Äî Street Play

Day 3 ‚Äî School Quiz

Day 4 ‚Äî Door-to-door Visits

Volunteers visited households to reinforce messages and help with queries.

Day 6 ‚Äî Monitoring Adoption

Day 7 ‚Äî Campaign Wrap-up

Conclusion

The project successfully set up community monitoring, introduced household treatment and safe storage practices, protected water sources and conducted awareness campaigns. Local volunteers are now equipped to continue monitoring and maintain safe drinking water practices for the village.
